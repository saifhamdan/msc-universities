# Germany

Germany is the first country documented in this repository because it aligns well with my current priorities: public universities, comparatively manageable tuition, strong technical reputation, and a serious academic environment in computer science, engineering, and informatics.

## Why Germany is the current focus

- Strong public university system.
- Good reputation in engineering, informatics, mathematics, and applied research.
- A realistic mix of prestigious and affordable institutions.
- Better fit for someone who already has strong practical experience and wants more theory.
- Broad enough landscape that I do not need to rely only on the most famous 3 to 5 schools.

## What I am evaluating

I am not optimizing for ranking alone. The current Germany research uses these criteria together:

- CS or IT relevance.
- Academic strength and general reputation.
- Tuition and semester contribution level.
- Living-cost pressure in the city.
- English vs German language requirements.
- Application timing and whether the program is actually available for the semester I want.
- Personal fit based on my background and long-term goals.

## University tier system

Each university is assigned a tier using five criteria scored together:

1. **CS / Informatics research strength** — research output, informatics-specific identity, academic recognition in the field.
2. **International profile** — visibility outside Germany, presence of English-medium programs, global ranking signal.
3. **English program availability** — confirmed or highly likely English-taught master's in a relevant field.
4. **Cost feasibility** — combined fee and city-cost pressure relative to the value of the program.
5. **Systems or backend-engineering fit** — relevance of available programs to a distributed-systems, backend, or platform-engineering background.

| Tier  | What it means                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **A** | Meets 4–5 criteria strongly. Clear CS or Informatics research identity, international recognition, English path confirmed or very likely, cost is manageable or justified by the academic gain. |
| **B** | Meets 3–4 criteria. Strong university with one notable caveat: high cost, language constraint, or program fit still uncertain.                                                                  |
| **C** | Meets 2–3 criteria. Respectable institution with multiple open questions about English availability, program match, or practical feasibility.                                                   |
| **D** | One or more deal-breakers present: German-only teaching confirmed, applied-sciences mismatch with theory goals, or very high cost without offsetting academic value.                            |

**Lang req** in the tracker refers to the language level noted in the original research (B2 or C1). Where the program is English-medium, this is an English proficiency threshold. Where the program is German-medium, it applies to German. Most entries still need direct verification — treat these as signals, not confirmed facts.

My current English qualification is TOEFL iBT 89, which meets B2 thresholds. Programs requiring C1 in English typically ask for TOEFL 90 or higher — this is a borderline concern for C1-listed entries.
